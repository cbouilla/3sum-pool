What is this?

This is a project to crowdsource a large cryptologic computation amongst bitcoin miners.

The combined computational power of bitcoin miners is amazing, but it's only for mining bitcoins. However, we can use it to find some kind of collision in a secure hash function.

Why do this?

For fun, and science!

We want to show that the computationnal power of cryptocurrency mining equipment could be used for cryptanalytic purposes. We think that forging a 128-bit partial collision on SHA-256 proves our point.

Who we are?

We are a group of french academic researchers in cryptology:

  • Charles Bouillaguet (University of Lille-1)
  • Claire Delaplace (IRISA)
  • Pierre-Alain Fouque (University of Rennes-1)

How does it work?

The 3SUM-pool is a special not-for-profit bitcoin mining pool. Miners participating in this pool contribute their power to the forgery of a (partial) hash collision.
